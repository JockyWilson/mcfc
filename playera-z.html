Alan Beck
Always the first name on the teamsheet (apart from The Kaiser, that's contractual) Alan has been a loyal servant to the team over the years, ever reliable at left back, his ability to marshall the troops with an accent 'slightly too posh to be heard on a football pitch' has become the stuff of legend. Whenever The Gaffer has been absent Al has often been the one to leap into his britches and grab the bull by the horns. MCFC Awards : Best ever moustache
Alan Heller
Alan "Ten Jags" Heller played in the inaugral MC 5-a-side debacle when he famously refused to buy a pair of trainers and instead played in a pair of cycling-come-hiking boots. His performance on the day failed to win him a long-term contract and he never played again. Alan has been working at Music Choice since 1993 and is still there today.

Alastair
The Lord Lucan of MCFC, played in the first M.A.L match and then disappeared never to be seen again. Signed from Lochgelly Albert he was an integral part of MCFC's groundbreaking "no possession" style of play. There have been numerous "sightings" of Alastair over the years, the Tesco Metro in Bromley is a particular hot-spot for fanatics anxious to confirm that their hero is still with us, maybe we'll never know what really happened.

Now residing in Canada this industrious left sided player was a mainstay during the early years. Deployed in both defence and midfield, Alex didn't only get a game because he's a Spurs fan.
